Education

Astronaut candidates must meet the following minimum qualifications: U.S. citizenship A master's degree in a STEM field (science, technology, engineering, or mathematics). At least two years of related professional experience or at least 1,000 hours flight hours. The ability to pass NASA's long-duration spaceflight physical Candidates who meet the minimum qualifications are then evaluated based on their experience, skills, and other factors. The selection process is highly competitive, and only a small number of candidates are chosen to become astronauts.
